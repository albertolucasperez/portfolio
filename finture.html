Finture

An app specially designed for millennials to learn everything about investments in a new way and take control of their financial future.

Overview

Investments made easy for millennials

Intro

Finture is the fourth project I completed from the UX/UI Design Bootcamp at Ironhack. I started from an initial brief where I was told to design a digital tool that would solve a finance or insurance problem.

From this premise I decided to investigate about future pensions of young people from Spain, since it is a topic that has been a subject of political and media debates in recent years.

Problem Statement

Millennials need a new learning and investing digital tool focused on secure financial assets to secure their long-term financial future.

Discover

Finance & investment habits

First of all, I focused the desk research on discovering the real forecasts of future pensions, as well as how millennials can protect themselves against this issue and what solutions are currently available for them.

Later, I carried out a series of surveys and interviews to determine what are the saving and investment habits of millennials and their level of knowledge about financial assets.

Define

Insights & personas

After conducting initial research, I synthesized all the gathered information into an Affinity Diagram to identity themes, opportunities, and features that Finture could improve upon. Afterwards I built an User Persona, User Journey and Empathy Map to help me focus on solving users pain points, frustrations and goals.

Research Insights

01.

According to the European Commission, those who retire in 2050 will receive only half of their last salary.

02.

79% of millennials think that their future pensions will be worse than the current ones.

03.

72% of millennials don’t invest any of the money they save.

04.

Only 6.1% of millennials invest in shares and 3.8% in investment funds.

05.

79% of millennials have thought about saving for the future pension.

06.

The number one reason millennials don't invest is ignorance.

Test

User Testing & metrics

Due to the short period of time available in this project, I have only been able to conduct user testing on Lo-Fi and Mid-Fi prototypes. However, with more time I would have wanted to carry out quantitive testing to measure metrics such as:

  • Measure the type of frustration of our users.
  • Determine the average time it takes users to complete a given level.
  • Experiment if push notifications promote user interaction in our app.
  • Increase in the percentage of millennials that invest in financial assets.
  • Estimate number of downloads of our app that justifies the increase in user interest.

Conclusion

What did I learn?

One of the challenges that I have faced during this project was working solo, since it has been the first individual project of the bootcamp. I think that having worked independently on this project has allowed me to value the importance of teamwork and discover how truly enriching it is to work in a group.

In addition, the second challenge that I had faced in this project was the technical nature of financial assets, since it is a complex subject that requires in-depth research. Despite the short period of time that we had in each of the bootcamp projects, I believe that I have been able to collect and synthesize the most important information to support my decisions and designs.

For future iterations of the project, I have the following in mind:

  1. Continue to develop new levels.
  2. Expand technical training in investment.
  3. Test and iterate with real users.
