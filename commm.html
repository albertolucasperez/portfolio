Overview

Trade skills for time with your community

Intro

Commm is the final project I completed, together with my colleague Eva Martin, from the UX/UI Design Bootcamp at Ironhack. We can proudly say that it was selected for the Ironhack Hackshow as one of the best two projects of the bootcamp.

This time we had complete freedom to choose the topic, so we decided to conduct an initial general research where we discovered some problems that could be the subject of our project. From there we agree that the problem that we were going to tackle was the lack of relatives to ask for help that a lot of people face nowadays.

Problem Statement

People need a digital tool that allows them to exchange their skills and knowledge to feel part of the community and/or address their problems without the need of money.

Discover

Favors & community feeling

After conducting an initial research, we continued with the desk research focusing on number of people and statistics in Spain based on INE (Spanish Statistics National Institute) data.

Next, we carried out a series of surveys and interviews to determine the most common type of relationship between neighbors, the percentage of people having difficulty asking for help and the motivation behind doing favors. We also wanted to know if people have pending problems and why.

Research Goals

01.

What type of relationship do people have with their neighbors?

02.

What percentage of people belong to a neighborhood association?

03.

What percentage of people have difficulty asking for help from relatives?

04.

What motivates people to do favors?

05.

What percentage of people have pending problems?

06.

Why don’t people solve these problems?

Desk Research
Surveys & Interviews

We had the opportunity to carry out 156 surveys and 10 interviews that helped us learn more about relationships between neighbors and what motivate people to do favors.

Competitive Analysis

We also decided to conduct a competitive analysis to identify competitor’s strengths and weaknesses to make informed decisions about Commm design, such as what features to include and how to differentiate from competitors creating a unique value proposition. It was not an easy task due to the lack of digital alternatives in the field of study.

Define

Insights & personas

After conducting research, we synthesized all the gathered information into a Thematic Analysis to identity themes, opportunities, and features that Commm could improve upon. Afterwards we built two User Personas, User Journeys to help us focus on solving users pain points, frustrations and goals.

Research Insights

01.

1.565.000 Spanish people DO NOT have relatives, friends or neighbors that they could ask for help.

02.

4.733.000 Spanish people consider it very difficult to get help from neighbors in case of need.

03.

81% of people consider it very important to have people from their neighborhood to ask for help in case of need.

04.

92% of people don’t belong to any neighborhood association.

05.

66% of people have a problem due to financial reason.

06.

The number one reason motivates people to do favors is to help a relative.

Ideate

Finding a digital alternative

To kick-off the design process, How Might We questions helped us brainstorm ideas to establish main features and elements that we then prioritize using an Impact & Effort canvas. Afterwards we designed two User Flow and initial sketches following the Crazy 8 technique to focus on the core elements of each screen.

How Might We

01.

How might we improve the positive feeling of doing favors?

02.

How might we help users have to time to do favors?

03.

How might we mitigate users’ pending problems to reduce their stress?

04.

How might we offer help to users in stressful situations when they have no one to turn to?

05.

How might we condense the information and make it more accessible?

06.

How might we encourage people to participate in the community?

Prototype

Developing a digital experience

Continuing with the design process, we developed the initial Lo-Fi prototype of the app based on both User Flows and sketches of the previous step. Using these first wireframes we conducted user testing, which helped us iterate on the solution based on the feedback and then developed a Mid-Fi prototype.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

Based on the sketches made following the Crazy 8 approach, we decided to make a Lo-Fi prototype using Figjam instead of drawing it on paper, to try a different technique.

Design

A neobrutalism design system

Finally, with all the gathered feedback and insights from the first two prototypes we designed and built a Style Guide and Hi-Fi prototype as the final solution for the Commm project. As we wanted to appeal to a younger audience, we went for a Neobrutalism design style that focus on simplicity but with a strong character by using bold and geometric shapes.

Conclusion

What did I learn?

The main challenge of this project was how to translate the activity of physical time banks into a digital experience such as a mobile app. After completing this project I have learnt how by leveraging the talents and expertise of others, we can work together to solve complex problems and create value for our communities.

Another challenged that we faced was the short period of time available to work on the project, as it had to be finished in less than two weeks. Due to this, I learnt how collaborating with others on the design process can be a valuable learning experience, as well as a great way to support each other working towards a the end product, result of team effort.

Overall, the final project of the UX/UI Design Bootcamp has helped me develop a deeper understanding of the design process and the role that collaboration and user-centered design can play in solving real-world problems. I am confident that these skills will be valuable assets as I continue to grow as a designer and work on future projects.

For future iterations of the project, we would like to:

  1. Add a categories section on the Home screen.
  2. Interactive map where users can find services, communities and other users.
  3. Translation of conversations.
  4. App rewards for users who collaborate with community management.
